A Reflection on Memes

Memes and Rhetorical Strategies

Throughout this week's activity, I was able to analyze some abstract rhetorical strategies. I spoke about the organizational structure of my chosen meme, and this is abstract because it is not a commonly thought of rhetorical strategy. I also spoke about pathos, which is a much more popular strategy. I believe that a rhetorical strategy is a very broad term, and there are many things that can be considered rhetorical strategies. This applies to concepts that have never been thought of before. I think my perception of memes has changed as I have realized that some memes can have true meaning pulled from them. These meaningful memes might not be very funny, but they can convey a message. In comparison, there are memes that are purely meant to be funny, and viewers will typically not get any message from these types of memes. The Revision Process

I did receive one piece of negative feedback from my partner. Since I included two memes in my assignment, she became confused by the second meme because she didn't know if it was being analyzed. I think this confusion is caused because people tend to look at images before reading the text on a page. I tried to use the second meme to support another point I was making, and I explained this in the text. I also wrote a caption for the meme saying that it was not part of the analysis. Next time I still would like to include 2 or more images in my analysis to make it more multimodal, but I will try to be more clear about why it is being included. When reviewing my partner's meme, I was familiar with the general format of the meme. It was a common image with text. The meme was about how students become shy when they have to present in class. I can relate to this because I become somewhat nervous before presenting, but I think my presentation skills are still above average.
